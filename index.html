Gender Determination with Morphometry of Eyes

Computer Vision, Image Analysis, TensorFlow, Keras, Deep Learning, Docker

In my deep learning project, I focused on analyzing patients' chest X-ray images to develop a computer vision model capable of predicting the images into three distinct classes: benign, pneumonia, or Covid-19. To accomplish this, I utilized popular frameworks such as TensorFlow and Keras for model training, TF-serving and Flask for model deployment, and Docker for containerization. By leveraging these frameworks, I ensured efficient and robust development, deployment, and scalability of the model.

Additionally, I embarked on an anthropometric analysis of the human face, which plays a crucial role in craniofacial plastic and reconstructive surgeries. Factors such as age, gender, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, environment, and region influence facial anthropometrics. Plastic surgeons rely on the anatomical dimensions of facial structures for repairing and reconstructing facial deformities. These dimensions, along with cultural, personality, and ethnic background factors, significantly contribute to facial appearance and aesthetics, with eye appearance and symmetry being particularly influential.

In this project, my objective was to build a model capable of scanning an image of a patient's eye and accurately predicting their gender as male or female. To achieve this, I employed the ResNet50 pre-trained model as the foundation of my deep learning convolutional neural network (CNN). The CNN model was trained using a dataset of 7,377 images belonging to the two gender classes and validated with an additional 1,843 images (20%) from the same classes.

To optimize the model's performance, I fine-tuned various parameters such as learning rate, inner layer size, and dropout rate. The resulting model achieved an impressive validation accuracy of 97.3%, with precision, recall, and area under the curve metrics reaching approximately 96%. To ensure ease of deployment and utilization, I converted the best model into a TensorFlow Lite model. For enhanced reproducibility and scalability, I deployed the project code within a Docker container. This containerization approach encapsulates the necessary dependencies, libraries, and the trained model, enabling seamless deployment across different systems and environments. I have made the project code available on GitHub, fostering collaboration, knowledge-sharing, and further advancements in the field. Through these projects, I demonstrate my proficiency in leveraging cutting-edge technologies and methodologies in machine learning and computer vision, as well as my expertise in deploying models using Docker containers.

January 2023

Breast Cancer Ultrasound Image Classifier

Computer Vision, Image Analysis, TensorFlow, Keras, Deep Learning, Docker

The treatment options and outcomes for benign and malignant cancer differ significantly. Benign cancers are generally not life-threatening and can often be managed through surgery alone. On the other hand, malignant cancers pose a greater risk to life and typically necessitate a combination of treatment approaches, including surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy. Accurate identification of the cancer type is essential for developing an effective treatment plan and improving the likelihood of a successful outcome.

In this collaborative project, our aim is to train a deep learning model that can classify patients into two categories: benign or malignant cancer. The model leverages a dataset comprising over 9000 ultrasound images of both malignant and benign breast cancer cases. To achieve this, we employed pretrained neural networks for training and subsequently deployed the model to Dockerhub for ease of access and utilization.

Prediction of Appliance Energy Consumption

Regression, EDA, Scikit-learn, Flask, Docker

This project is an attempt to predict the energy consumption from appliances in a small building in Belgium. It involves performing exploratory data analysis (EDA) and feature engineering and extraction. After evaluating several regression algorithms, the gradient boosting regressor was chosen as the most suitable model. The deployed model achieved an RMSE of 0.367 and an R2 score of 0.699. The model was deployed using Flask and Docker for seamless integration and ease of use. The project code, including the model deployment, is available on GitHub for further exploration and collaboration.
